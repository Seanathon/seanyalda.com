# Sean Yalda — Portfolio

## Register

**Brand.** This site IS the product — a personal portfolio where design itself signals craft. Not a SaaS shell; not a dashboard. Marketing/editorial-shaped pages with photography and long-form case studies.

## Users

**Primary:** Hiring managers and senior engineers / designers evaluating Sean for senior roles in education, AI, and platform engineering. Often arriving from a referral, LinkedIn, or an active job application. On desktop most of the time; mobile after that. Scanning quickly first, then drilling into one or two case studies.

**Secondary:** Past collaborators, peers re-finding context. Potential clients for consulting / commissions. Photo print enquiries.

## Product Purpose

Concentrate twelve years of full-stack engineering and design work into a small set of pages a reviewer can absorb in five minutes. Show range — backend infrastructure, AI orchestration, UX, photography — without flattening it into a list. Make the work do the talking; let craft do the rest.

## Brand Personality

- **Confident, not loud.** The writing is tight; the design is restrained until it isn't.
- **Editorial, not corporate.** Closer to a print monograph than a SaaS landing page.
- **Photographic.** Real photos (Sean's own) carry mood and place — not stock illustration.
- **Multi-theme as craft signal.** Eleven hand-tuned color themes the visitor can swap. The default is grounded; the alternates demonstrate range. Theme is expressive, not chaotic — every alternate was deliberate.
- **Hybrid voice.** The home and case studies carry a punchy resume register; the about page is warmer and more discursive. Both belong to the same person.

## Anti-References

- Generic "agency template" portfolios (centered hero, identical card grid of 6 projects, gradient buttons).
- SaaS marketing landing pages dressed up as portfolios (3-up feature grid, hero-metric stat block).
- "Brutalist" portfolios that confuse hostility for distinctiveness.
- Portfolios that hide the work behind interaction (sliders, parallax-only navigation, "click to reveal").

## Strategic Design Principles

1. **Photography is the brand.** Hero photos are real places (Bryce, Tetons, Iceland, caves). They set mood per page; they're never decorative or stock.
2. **Typography over chrome.** Heavy display headlines and clear hierarchy do the visual work. Avoid card-decoration patterns (drop shadows, gradient borders).
3. **Theme is content, not chrome.** The notch toolbar exposes themes as a deliberate craft surface — switchable per visitor, persisted across pages.
4. **Density at the right grain.** Case studies use dense bulleted points where they help (counter-numbered enumerations, side-by-side galleries). Marketing surfaces stay airy.
5. **Static-first, animated where it earns it.** GSAP scroll-reveals are universal; motion beyond that is reserved for transitions, photo interactions, and per-element accents that genuinely improve comprehension.
