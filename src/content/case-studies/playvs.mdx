<CaseSection eyebrow="Context" title="Esports for high schools, built from zero.">
  PlayVS built the infrastructure and platform for high school and collegiate esports — partnering with game publishers and high school governing bodies to operate sanctioned leagues across the country. I joined as the first employee, helping CEO Delane Parnell raise the seed round with Science Inc., designing the MVP, and producing fundraising pitch decks for the $15M Series A.

  Through the $30M Series B I led product engineering as Technical Product Manager. Through the $50M Series C, I drove growth and marketing engineering and scaled the platform from zero to tens of thousands of student users nationally.
</CaseSection>

<CaseSection variant="cream" eyebrow="The challenge" title="10–20 marketing initiatives a quarter.">
  The growth team executed 10 to 20 initiatives per quarter — Academy, Game Changers, summer and winter tournaments, regional marketing pages — and needed a web solution that could keep up. The site had to evolve constantly without becoming bottlenecked by the engineering team's capacity.

  <CasePoints items={[
    "Architect a sitemap flexible enough for current programs and a year of unknowns.",
    "Design a content model whose components match the editorial system.",
    "Help users in an unfamiliar industry navigate naturally.",
    "Define metrics so we'd know whether we were on track."
  ]} />
</CaseSection>

<CaseSection eyebrow="The approach" title="Component-driven, content-first.">
  A component-based architecture and content model that could grow alongside the company without massive overhead each time an initiative launched. Using Prismic, non-technical employees could update the site on the fly — and we built a platform that could adapt to the ever-changing needs of a fast-paced startup in a new industry.
</CaseSection>

<CaseSection variant="cream" eyebrow="Information architecture" title="80% SaaS site, 20% community portal.">
  We applied the Pareto principle to frame the site as roughly 80% SaaS marketing and 20% community-focused. That single decision compressed the discovery work and let the team execute against a clear editorial mandate.

  <CasePoints items={[
    "Sitemap built for present programs and future initiatives.",
    "Components scoped to the content model — not the other way around.",
    "Navigation tested with real users from this new industry.",
    "Metrics defined alongside every initiative."
  ]} />
</CaseSection>

<CaseSection eyebrow="Web development management" title="Adaptive engineering for a moving target.">
  An all-JavaScript stack (Next.js + Node.js), headless CMS with decoupled frontend, version control, a CI/CD pipeline on AWS Elastic Beanstalk, and project management tooling — built so the team could adapt to constantly shifting needs and budget.

  <CasePoints items={[
    "Cost/value analysis to ship the highest-impact items first.",
    "Partner (UA5) that could flex with budget and timeline.",
    "System designed for the company, its partners, and its community.",
    "Strategy → design → development → launch with a people-first mindset."
  ]} />
</CaseSection>

<CaseSection variant="cream" eyebrow="Frontend engineering" title="~30 components, a huge surface area.">
  Using Prismic's REST API and the PlayVS product API, we produced a large website with minimal backend code. The site comprised roughly 30 components that could be composed into landing pages, microsites, and more on the fly — ultimately empowering the growth team to ship freely.
